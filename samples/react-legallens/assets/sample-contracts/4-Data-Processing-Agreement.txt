DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

This DPA is entered into as of September 15, 2024, forming part of the Master Services Agreement between:

HealthCare Systems International Inc., Chicago, IL 60611 ("Data Controller")
AND
SecureCloud Processing Services Ltd., Dublin, Ireland ("Data Processor")

§1. SCOPE AND PURPOSE

Data Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of Data Controller for cloud infrastructure and healthcare data processing services. Processing must comply with GDPR, CCPA, and all applicable data protection laws.

Types of Personal Data:
- Patient identification (names, addresses, DOB, ID numbers)
- Health and medical records
- Healthcare provider information
- Insurance and billing data
- Contact information (email, phone)

Data Subjects: Patients, healthcare providers, insurance beneficiaries, legal guardians

§2. DATA PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

§2.1 Processing Instructions
Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller or as required by law. Notify Controller if instruction violates data protection laws.

§2.2 Confidentiality
Ensure all personnel are bound by confidentiality obligations.

§2.3 Security Measures
Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures:
- Encryption and pseudonymization
- System confidentiality, integrity, availability, resilience
- Regular testing and vulnerability assessment
- Incident recovery capabilities

§2.4 Sub-processors
General authorization granted for Sub-processors listed at www.securecloud.com/subprocessors. 30 days notice for new Sub-processors. Controller may object on reasonable data protection grounds within 15 days.

§2.5 Data Subject Rights
Assist Controller in responding to requests for:
- Access, rectification, erasure
- Restriction of processing
- Data portability
- Right to object

§2.6 Security Incidents
Notify Controller within 24 hours of Security Incident discovery with:
- Nature of incident
- Categories and number of affected Data Subjects
- Likely consequences
- Mitigation measures taken

§2.7 Return or Deletion
At Controller election, delete or return all Personal Data after service ends. Continue compliance if retention required by law.

§2.8 Audit Rights
Controller may audit once annually (30 days notice) during business hours. Controller responsible for costs. Additional audits permitted after Security Incident or by Supervisory Authority request.

§3. DATA TRANSFERS

Transfers outside EEA only if: (a) adequate protection exists; (b) Standard Contractual Clauses in place; or (c) GDPR Article 49 derogation applies.

§4. LIABILITY

Data Processor liable for damages only if failed to comply with processor-specific obligations or acted outside/contrary to lawful instructions. Data Processor indemnifies Controller for breaches of DPA or data protection law violations.

§5. TERM

Effective September 15, 2024 until MSA termination. Sections on deletion, liability, and general provisions survive termination.

§6. GOVERNING LAW

Governed by laws of Ireland (to extent not inconsistent with Data Protection Laws). This DPA prevails over MSA for Personal Data processing.
